# Hlin Trollbane — Lore & Voice

> Prologue guest unit (the cold-open's must-survive lead). **Mechanics live in
> [`chapters/ch00-prologue-a-dagger-of-ice.yaml`](../chapters/ch00-prologue-a-dagger-of-ice.yaml).**
> Recurs as quest-giver/patron from ch01 (she hires the seven at The Northlook).

## Concept

Elderly shield dwarf, retired bounty hunter — neutral good, a nasty scar across her nose,
rarely seen without her pipe (book printed p.22). Plied her trade up and down the Sword
Coast and made "a few too many enemies," which is why she wound up in Icewind Dale. Out of
boredom and moral decency she's been investigating the dagger-of-ice murders because no
one else — not even the Council of Speakers — can be bothered. Too old and frail to take
a killer alone; sharp enough to have found him anyway.

- **Wants:** cold, swift justice; Ten-Towns kept safe; to matter despite her age.
- **Fears (unspoken):** being too worn for the work that defines her.

## Voice

Plain, declarative, dry. A detective laying out facts, not a storyteller. Her book
monologue (printed p.23) is the calibration sample — quote-mine it freely.

**Diction rules**
- Short factual sentences; she counts things ("the only three towns that…").
- Hedged inference, never certainty: "Maybe, just maybe…", "so I'm guessing…"
- Understated irony as the punchline: "Kiss of the Frostmaiden, indeed."
- Money is plain business: "I've more gold than I need."
- Pipe-pause ellipses sparingly; exclamation points almost never.

**Calibration lines (book-verbatim or near)**
- "What struck me is how comfortable Sephek looked in this weather. No coat, no scarf,
  no gloves. It was like the cold couldn't touch him."
- "Maybe, just maybe, Sephek is doing the Frostmaiden's work."
- "I followed Torg's for a tenday as it moved from town to town. Quite the devious
  little enterprise, but that's not my concern."

**Banned:** poetic metaphor, courtly speech, modern slang, panic. Even shaken (the
prologue ending), she processes in facts.
