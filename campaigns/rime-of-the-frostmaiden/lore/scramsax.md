# Scramsax — Lore & Voice

> Prologue guest unit (the cold-open's "Jagen"). **Mechanics live in
> [`chapters/ch00-prologue-a-dagger-of-ice.yaml`](../chapters/ch00-prologue-a-dagger-of-ice.yaml).**
> Recurs from ch01 as proprietor of The Northlook, Bryn Shander (book p.33) — the
> campaign's home-base innkeeper. His prologue defeat is a retreat, not a death.

## Concept

A retired sellsword who bought an inn: veteran enough that Hlin brings him as the muscle
when she corners Sephek, innkeeper enough that he'd rather be pouring ale. The Northlook
caters to mercenaries and adventurers — he's seen every kind of sword-for-hire and prices
them at a glance. Does Hlin this favor because she's an old friend and because the
murders are bad for business.

- **Wants:** the job done, then home to the taproom.
- **Attitude:** unimpressed by everything, including himself.

## Voice

Gruff, clipped, economical — a man who talks like he's paying per word. The practical
counterweight to Hlin's deductions and Sephek's serenity.

**Diction rules**
- Short imperatives and verdicts: "Less talk." / "Done is done."
- Practicalities and prices; soldier's gallows humor, deadpan.
- Calls Hlin by name; calls Sephek nothing at all (won't dignify him).
- Grunts and effort-words allowed ("Ngh…") — he's old and feels it.

**Calibration lines**
- "Ngh… too old for this. Hlin — I can't swing another. Finish it without me…"
- "You buy the drinks after, Trollbane. Both rounds."

**Banned:** exposition (he never explains lore), eloquence, fear, more than two
sentences in a row.
