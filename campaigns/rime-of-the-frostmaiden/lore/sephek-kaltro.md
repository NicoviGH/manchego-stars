## Concept

Outwardly: a charming caravan guard for Torg's trading company — makes friends easily,
good with a blade, comfortable in lethal cold. No coat, no scarf, no gloves. Truthfully
(book p.23-24): a drowned mariner's body inhabited by a frost-druid spirit in Auril's
service, executing Ten-Towners who cheat the sacrifice lotteries. He claims he was born
on the winter solstice with the Frostmaiden's blessing. He is smug about all of it: he
has found a higher purpose as Auril's executioner. He detests anything warmer than a
distant torch.

- **Wants:** to do her work; to be near the cold; to be *seen* as blessed, not damned.
- **Never:** angry, afraid, loud. Defeat amuses him — the cold always comes back.

## Voice

Calm, unhurried, faintly liturgical when Auril comes up. He talks to victims the way a
priest talks to a congregation: with serene, pitying certainty. The menace is in the
warmth of his manner, not threats.

**Diction rules**
- Even, complete sentences; never raises his voice, never curses.
- Auril in reverent epithets: "her work", "her due", "the Frostmaiden's kiss".
- Cold imagery only — ice, rime, breath, stillness. Fire words never pass his lips.
- Frames murder as bookkeeping or mercy: "balanced the scales", "what is owed".
- Pity for his enemies, amusement at their effort.

**Calibration lines**
- "You cut the ice… not the cold that made it…"
- "I was born on the winter solstice. Her blessing has been on me ever since."
  (adapted from Torrga's account of him)
- "They cheated her of what was owed. I only balanced the scales."

**Imagery budget (don't spend early)**
- Prologue escape: blade crumbles to shards, body rimes over, gone in a breath of
  frost — *no corpse*.
- Final death (payoff chapter): the glamour fails — the handsome man replaced by a
  shriveled, drowned-mariner corpse, as if pulled from an ancient tomb. Reserved.

**Banned:** shouting, crude insults, fear, fire metaphors, contractions when speaking
of Auril (small formality shift marks the liturgy).
