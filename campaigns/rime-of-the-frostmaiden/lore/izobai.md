# Izobai — Lore & Voice

Ch1 "The Iron Trail" boss. A female goblin warlord out of Karkolohk (book canon),
holed up in the old waystation on the Bryn Shander trail. Her band ambushed the
dwarves' sled and took forty Battlehammer ingots — she's bolted the best of the scrap
into plate over her hide. Rides the vanilla Breguet boss slot (armor-knight; our
"magic beats armor" lesson) with a custom green-goblin bust.

## Concept

- **Wants:** the haul, and to keep it. Iron, coin, anything with a price.
- **Is:** a cunning, mercenary thief-warlord — pragmatic, not a fanatic. She has no
  stake in Auril or the endless winter; she's just the first opportunist the party
  meets profiting off the chaos. She'd take a bribe over a fight, but never gives up
  what's already hers.
- **Never:** zealous, noble, or self-sacrificing. Defeat to her is a bad deal, not a
  tragedy — she dies counting the cost.

## Voice

Cunning and mocking, transactional. She appraises everyone like loot and prices out
their motives. Dry, dark humor at the party's expense. The menace is in how cheaply
she values them.

**Diction rules**
- Everything has a price; she frames the world in coin, cost, and value.
- Mocks the party's motives — working a dead dwarf's errand for a handful of coin.
- Possessive of what she's taken: "mine," "hers," "under my boots."
- Clear, clipped speech — a warlord, NOT a feral pidgin. (The sibilant "ironses"
  goblin-cant was considered and rejected; she's smarter than that.)
- Contractions, plain words; she's blunt, not eloquent.

**Calibration lines**
- Turn-1 taunt: "The iron's right here, under my boots. Come and take it."
- Death (0x961): "Should've taken the gold... and run..."

**Banned:** liturgy / Auril-reverence (she's no cultist), the "-ses" goblin pidgin,
heroic or noble register, fear or pleading. She mocks; she does not beg.
