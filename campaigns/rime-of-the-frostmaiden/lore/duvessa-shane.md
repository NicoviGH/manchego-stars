# Duvessa Shane — Lore & Voice

> Speaker of Bryn Shander and recurring patron. First appears in the **ch01 ending
> scene** (`chapters/ch01-the-iron-trail.yaml` → `events[chapter_end]`), where she hires
> the company for ongoing Ten-Towns work and points them west to Targos. Non-combat NPC;
> rides a guest face slot (`GUEST_PORTRAIT_MAP`). Book grounding: printed p.32–33
> ("Bryn Shander in a Nutshell" + the bust on printed p.33).

## Concept

The honest face of the capital. **Lawful good human noble, relatively young** — head of
the Council of Speakers despite her age, and she feels the weight of it. Earnest,
plain-spoken, leads by sincerity rather than rank. Knows every name in Bryn Shander and
buries too many of them; two years of an endless winter have worn her without hardening
her. She hires by results, not pedigree — the iron job was her audition for the seven.

- **Wants:** Ten-Towns kept alive through the winter; capable hands she can trust.
- **Fears (unspoken):** that the cold outlasts everyone she's responsible for.

Likeness (book bust, printed p.33): dark center-parted hair with a short straight fringe,
freckled cold-flushed face, deep-blue coat with a heavy white-grey fur collar.

## Voice

Warm, plain, a little tired. Sincere where Hlin is dry — she means every word, and she
says them like someone used to being listened to but never lording it. Leads with thanks,
not titles.

**Diction rules**
- Plain, complete sentences; civic register without council-floor stiffness.
- Credits people by what they did, concretely ("what three of my guards couldn't").
- States the stakes plainly, no poetry: deaths, weeks, roads, the work that needs doing.
- Names the deal straight — hire, pay, where to go next — like an honest employer.
- Tired-but-steady, never grim-clipped and never gushing. No exclamation-point warmth.
- Uses "we" for the town, "I" for the hire — the responsibility is personal.

**Calibration lines**
- "You brought the iron back, and the dwarves who hauled it home. That's more than I'd
  hoped for."
- "I won't pretend it's the last of the trouble. It's the first of it."
- "Targos has had frost druids in the streets, and folk listening to them. Go west. See
  what's taken root there."

**Banned:** courtly/formal stiffness, council-jargon, grim one-word fatalism, gushing or
exclamatory praise, menace, salesman patter, modern slang.
