# Vellynne Harpell — Lore & Voice

> Arcane Brotherhood necromancer and recurring NPC. First appears in the **ch02 opening
> scene** (`chapters/ch02-cold-welcome.yaml` → `events[chapter_start]`), stopping the party
> at the Bryn Shander west gate to set them on the trail of her stolen orb (Professor Skant
> — see [`frostmaiden-voices.md`](frostmaiden-voices.md)). Recurs through the Brotherhood
> arc (Sunblight onward) as a pragmatic, self-interested ally. Non-combat NPC; rides a guest
> face slot (`GUEST_PORTRAIT_MAP`). Book/canon grounding: appendix C stat block (printed
> p.311) + the Arcane Brotherhood sidebar (printed p.162); cross-checked against Forgotten
> Realms canon (necromancer, late-blooming to magic in her forties; a nervous-system tremor
> affecting gait and balance — which is why she rides, never walks).

## Concept

The cordial face of a cold guild — and the most dangerous kind of ally: useful, honest
about being self-interested, and always a move ahead. A **Harpell of Longsaddle** (an
eccentric, famous wizard line), underestimated her whole life, who took up necromancy late
and is racing three Brotherhood rivals across Icewind Dale for the secrets under the ice.
A body tremor means she travels seated on a small sled drawn not by dogs but by **reanimated
kobolds**. She does not threaten and she does not gush; she assesses, and she hires.

- **Wants:** her stolen orb back; to reach what the winter is hiding before her rivals do.
- **Method:** cultivate capable people as instruments — deal straight, pay on delivery.
- **Not:** cruel for sport (that is Avarice), nor warm. Cold, precise, transactional.

## Voice

Cold, formal, economical. A scholar's precision with none of the warmth — she means her
words but spends as few as possible. Quiet authority, never a raised voice; she expects to
be obeyed because she is used to being right. Treats strangers as assets the moment they're
useful. The opposite of a thug: the menace, where it exists, is in how little she needs to
say.

**Diction rules**
- Measured, complete sentences, then nothing extra — no pleasantries, no preamble.
- Frames people as instruments: "I've a use for that," "you'll do," "reach it before she does."
- Transactional: states the task, the pay, the deadline. Numbers are discussed on delivery, not before.
- Competitive undercurrent — she is racing peers and says so flatly: "I don't finish second."
- Refers to the other Brotherhood wizards as colleagues/rivals, not monsters — guild, not villainy.
- Her tremor is *shown, never said* — she stills her hands, stays seated, rides rather than walks; she never explains or excuses it.
- FE8 cadence (see [`frostmaiden-voices.md`](frostmaiden-voices.md)): short hard-broken lines, a `...` beat before a cold turn, `--` for the clipped pivot.

**Calibration lines**
- "A moment. You're for the west road -- and I've a use for that."
- "It speaks, and it does not know when to stop. It's mine. Bring it back, and you'll be paid."
- "Enough. But you aren't the only ones on this road -- and I don't finish second."

**Banned:** warmth or pleasantries, loud threats / thuggish menace, villain theatrics
(cackling, gloating), cruelty for its own sake, self-pity or any spoken reference to her
condition, rambling monologue, modern slang.
